🌟 Module 25 – Noun/Verb Stress Pt. 1 and Pt. 2

This module explores how English stress changes based on word form. You’ll work on noun/verb pairs where the same spelling has different pronunciation depending on stress, and then go deeper with words that end in -ate and change their vowel sound and stress pattern.

🎯 Your Weekly Practice Tracker

📘 Focus Areas This Module

  • ✔️ First-syllable stress for nouns (e.g., record, permit, present)
  • ✔️ Second-syllable stress for verbs (e.g., reCORD, perMIT, preSENT)
  • ✔️ Words ending in -ate (e.g., graduate, separate, delegate)
  • ✔️ Vowel shift between /eɪ/ and /ɪ/ in different forms

📚 This Module’s Lessons

🔹 Part 1 – Noun/Verb Pairs
Learn how words like “record,” “permit,” and “object” change meaning based on stress. Practice identifying and pronouncing each form with correct emphasis.

🔹 Part 2 – Words Ending in -ate
Explore how stress and vowel sounds shift in -ate endings. For example, 'graduate' as a noun is /ˈgrædʒuɪt/, but as a verb it’s /ˈgrædʒueɪt/. Practice clear differences in speaking.
