🌟 Module 26 – Articles: a, an, the

This module focuses on how to pronounce the articles “a,” “an,” and “the” in natural speech. You’ll practice when to reduce them, when to emphasize them, and how they sound in both casual and careful speech.

🎯 Your Weekly Practice Tracker

📘 Focus Areas This Module

  • ✔️ Article “a” as /ə/ (uh)
  • ✔️ Article “an” before vowel sounds
  • ✔️ Article “the” as /ðə/ or /ðiː/ depending on the sound after it
  • ✔️ Reducing or stressing articles in different situations

📚 This Module’s Lessons

🔹 Lesson 1 – Article “a”
Learn when and how to reduce the article “a” in phrases like “a book,” “a car,” and “a message.”

🔹 Lesson 2 – Article “an”
Practice using “an” before vowel sounds and pronouncing it smoothly in connected speech.

🔹 Lesson 3 – Article “the”
Understand the two pronunciations of “the” — /ðə/ before consonants and /ðiː/ before vowels — and how to use each naturally.
